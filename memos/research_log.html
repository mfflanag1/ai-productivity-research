1. W1: Quarterly speed test of the IT puzzle

Question: Does the CSD variance signal appear before the ~1995 IT-era TFP break at quarterly frequency where it is absent annually? Discriminates the unified paper Section 7 hypotheses: SPEED (signal exists, compressed below annual resolution) vs SCOPE (no signal at any frequency).

Pre-registered specification — LOCKED 2026-06-09, before any primary run

  • Series: dtfp_util (utilization-adjusted business-sector TFP growth, annualized %, =400·Δlog), sample 1947:Q2–2019:Q4 (pre-COVID truncation; full sample is a sensitivity).
  • Break date: Bai-Perron estimate nearest 1995 on cumulated log level of dtfp_util; fallback if no break found in 1993–2004: Fernald (2014) published 1995:Q4 treated as known break (fallback use will be logged).
  • Pre-break window: 120 quarters (30 yrs). EWS rolling window: 60 quarters (15 yrs). Identical time spans to the published annual analysis at 4x sampling density — the equivalence is the test.
  • Detrending: gaussian, AICc-selected bandwidth (record selected value).
  • Indicators: rolling variance and rolling lag-1 autocorrelation of detrended increments.
  • Inference: Kendall tau; AR(1) surrogate p (n=1000, seed=42) is the reportable p-value (naive Kendall p invalid with ~91 overlapping rolling points; shown for comparability only). One-sided (rising), alpha=0.05.
  • Annual mirror: publisher's own annual sheet (same xlsx), identical test at pre=30, ews=15.
  • Negative control: pre-1973:Q1 window at pre=100, ews=40 (only ~104 quarters exist; pre-specified deviation).
  • Secondary pre-specified hypotheses (own raw p, not grid members): (a) sectoral dtfp_I_util before its ~1995 break — sharpest predicted signal under SPEED; (b) variance-ratio variant var(TFP)/var(GDP growth) to net out Great Moderation/recession volatility common to both.
  • Sensitivity grid (BH-corrected within indicator family): series {dtfp, dtfp_util} × frequency {quarterly, annual} × pre {80,100,120}q / {20,25,30}y × ews {20,40,60}q / {10,15}y.
  • Interpretation map (decided up front): | Quarterly | Annual | MC power at quarterly | Verdict | |---|---|---|---| | signal | null | yes | SPEED confirmed — headline | | null | null | yes | SCOPE favored | | null | null | no | underpowered; lean on MC | | signal | signal | — | contradicts published annual null → investigate BCL-vs-Fernald data difference first |

2. W2: Real-time detector

Question: Paper A claims the GPT/non-GPT distinction is "detectable in real time" but never builds the detector. Operationalize it: a sliding-window, trailing-only (no look-ahead) CSD detector backtested over the full panel with ROC/AUC and false-alarm rates, validated out-of-sample on the steam transition, then applied to current data for AI. W1 conditions the interpretation: the detector can only announce an electrification-like (broad, slow) transition — an IT-like one produces no signal by construction.

Pre-registered specification — LOCKED 2026-06-09, before any backtest run

  • Detector at (country, t), trailing-only: last 30 TFP increments ending at t (require full window); gaussian detrend with AICc bandwidth fit on that window ONLY (no future data); rolling variance and AC(1) with 15-obs windows; Kendall tau of each indicator → scores var_tau(t), ac_tau(t). Mirrors the published 30/15 convention but computed causally.
  • Data/labels (backtest): BCL panel ≤2019 (paper convention); breaks = per-country Bai-Perron (max_breaks=5, trim 0.15), categories from classify_breaks (electrification 1910–1935 = GPT class). Positive label: country-years t with an electrification break at B and t ∈ [B−20, B). Negative: all other country-years (including pre-non-GPT-break years and WWII — that is the point). Lead sensitivity: 10 and 30 years.
  • Primary metric: AUC of var_tau (positives vs negatives), country-cluster bootstrap (1000 draws) for the CI. Secondary: ac_tau AUC, mean-rank combination.
  • Operating point: threshold = 90th percentile of var_tau among negatives (10% FPR by construction). Report: per-break hit rate (≥1 alarm in the 20y pre-break window) for GPT vs non-GPT breaks; lead-time distribution; false-alarm rate in quiet periods (no break within 20y).
  • Out-of-sample (historical): identical detector + BCL-calibrated threshold on Maddison log GDP/capita increments, GBR/FRA/NLD, 1700–1900. Success criterion: alarm clustering in the pre-steam window (1780–1830) above the quiet-period rate. No retuning.
  • Present-day application: (a) detector paths 1980–2019 for all BCL countries (primary; ≤2022 exploratory — BCL includes COVID years which are exogenous-shock turbulence, not CSD); (b) Fernald quarterly trailing detector (window 120q, ews 60q) — primary value at 2019:Q4, exploratory path to 2026:Q1 with 2020:Q1–2021:Q4 winsorized at the 1/99 pct of the pre-2020 distribution; (c) trailing 30y Kendall tau of cross-country dispersion (cross_std from compute_cross_sectional_dispersion) — the strongest published pre-electrification signal (τ=+0.56).
  • Interpretation guardrail (from W1): a quiet detector today does NOT mean no AI transition — it means no electrification-like approach is visible yet. State this wherever present-day results are reported.

Decision log

  • 2026-06-09 [DECISION] This is a fixed-rule detector, NOT a trained classifier. With effectively one GPT event in the BCL panel (electrification), any fitted model would train and test on the same event. The decision rule (tau thresholds) is taken from the published retrospective analysis; the only calibrated quantity is the alarm threshold, set mechanically at 10% FPR. Steam serves as the genuine out-of-sample event. Alternatives rejected: probit/ML on pooled panel (circular), leave-one-country-out (countries share the event, not independent).
  • 2026-06-09 [DECISION] Trailing gaussian detrending refit at every t: the published pipeline detrends the full window retrospectively; a real-time detector must not see the future. This makes backtest AUC an honest estimate of deployable performance.

  • Primary AUC (var_tau, lead=20): 0.628, cluster-bootstrap CI [0.433, 0.833] — above chance in point estimate, but the CI spans 0.5. ac_tau AUC 0.539 [0.411, 0.661]. Lead 10/30 nearly identical.

  • Present day: 1/23 BCL countries above threshold as of 2019 (0/23 as of 2022); US quarterly trailing var_tau −0.683 at 2019:Q4 (+0.078 through 2026:Q1 COVID-winsorized); cross-country dispersion trailing tau +0.039 at 2019. No electrification-like approach signature visible now.

  • 2026-06-09 [SURPRISE] The causal detector does NOT discriminate GPT from war breaks at a usable operating point — it fires before WWII breaks (0.75) and "other" breaks (0.70) MORE often than before electrification breaks (0.50). The published retrospective differential (group mean taus differ, p=0.002) is real but does not translate into single-indicator real-time GPT-specificity. Paper A's abstract claim "the distinction is detectable in real time" needs substantial qualification.
  • 2026-06-09 [SURPRISE] Retrospective vs causal gap on steam: the published retrospective steam taus were huge (+0.70 to +0.83, p<0.001) but the causal trailing detector barely fires (GBR: zero alarms in 1780–1830). 3. W3: Pillar-intensity identification

Question: Paper B's three-pillar decomposition fails (R² 0.77 but all pillar p > 0.20) because the pillar barriers share one dominant time trend. Rajan-Zingales-style fix: identify pillar effects from the INTERACTION of cross-technology intensity variation with pillar cost levels at launch, absorbing the common trend with a launch-year control. If it works, we learn WHICH input cost drives diffusion speed — the quantity an AI forecast actually needs. If it fails, N=22 cross-tech variation is insufficient and the mechanism stays unidentified (an honest strengthening of Paper B's existing caveat).

Pre-registered specification — LOCKED 2026-06-09, before any estimation

  • Intensity weights: the per-technology cognitive/energy/coordination weights in _TECH_CATALOG (src/paper_data_prep.py:33-167). Crucially these were fixed when Paper B was written, long before W3 was conceived — they cannot have been reverse-engineered to make this regression work.
  • Data: exact Paper B pipeline — classify_hccta_technologies → fit_logistic_by_country → aggregate_diffusion_speeds (k_median per technology); prepare_cost_panel_for_paper for raw pillar cost indices at launch year (NOT the barrier components, which embed task weights and era-level inheritance discounts — those are absorbed by the trend/era controls instead).
  • Primary specification (OLS, N≈22): log k_j = α + δ·(L_j − 1900)/100 + β_C·[w_jC·log p_C(L_j)] + β_E·[w_jE·log p_E(L_j)] + β_X·[w_jX·log p_X(L_j)] + ε_j The launch-year trend absorbs the common cost decline; β_p is identified only if technologies intensive in pillar p diffused systematically slower when that pillar was expensive at their launch, over and above the trend. Hypothesis: β_p < 0 for pillars that matter.
  • Inference (small N, no asymptotics): pairs bootstrap (2,000 draws) percentile CIs; placebo permutation test (1,000 draws) permuting the WEIGHT TRIPLES across technologies — if identification is real, breaking the tech-weight link must destroy the coefficients. Leave-one-out coefficient ranges.
  • Success criterion (per pillar): bootstrap 95% CI excludes 0 AND permutation p < 0.05 AND LOO sign-stable. Anything less → report as unidentified.
  • Sensitivities: era fixed effects instead of linear trend; drop mobile phones (Paper B's most influential obs); coarse primary-pillar dummy (argmax weight) × own-pillar log cost; no-trend variant (to reproduce/diagnose the original Paper B failure within the same dataset).

Decision log

  • 2026-06-09 [DECISION] Use raw pillar cost indices at launch interacted with catalog weights, not the compute_structural_barrier pillar components: the barrier components multiply in era-level inheritance discounts (steam 0/0/0, electricity .1/.2/.15, IT .4/.55/.4) that are collinear with any era control and would contaminate the interaction. Rejected alternative: country×technology panel with country-level historical pillar costs — the data do not exist before ~1900 outside a few cities.
  • 2026-06-09 [DECISION] Permutation statistic = STUDENTIZED coefficient (|t| with classical SEs inside each permuted fit), not the raw coefficient: under permutation the true signal moves into the residual and inflates raw-beta dispersion, costing power. Studentization pivots that out; the permutation itself supplies exactness. Decided on SYNTHETIC data (unit test exposed the power loss: known β_E=−2 recovered exactly but raw-beta permutation p=0.05) — locked before any real-data estimation. Raw-beta p reported alongside for transparency; add-one p-value correction used.

Results log

  • 2026-06-09 [RESULT] Full run complete (notebooks/17_pillar_identification.py, seeds=42). VERDICT: NO PILLAR IDENTIFIED — the mechanism behind the cost-speed elasticity remains unidentified, and the intensity-interaction design is now ruled out as a rescue at this sample size.
  • Dataset: 30 tech observations (24 distinct), 1605–1983, catalog weights merged with pillar costs at launch.
  • Diagnostic (no trend control): z_E −0.84 [−1.35, −0.51], z_X −0.85 [−1.57, −0.27] "significant" — but this is just the common cost trend reloading onto the pillars (Paper B's original problem).
  • PRIMARY (trend + interactions): z_C −0.07 [−0.39, +0.17] perm p=0.98; z_E −0.64 [−1.13, +1.08] perm p=0.37; z_X −0.43 [−1.33, +1.20] perm p=0.73. R²=0.57 (vs 0.54 trend-free simple barrier).
  • Sensitivities: era FE, drop-mobile, primary-pillar dummies — all CIs include 0 everywhere.
  • Leave-one-out: no pillar sign-stable.
  • Every pillar fails all three legs of the pre-registered criterion.

Surprises / anomalies

  • 2026-06-09 [SURPRISE] The deeper diagnosis: the catalog intensity weights provide too little INDEPENDENT variation — only 12 distinct weight triples among 30 observations, weights cluster by era (energy-heavy techs are old: corr(launch, w_C)=+0.36, corr(launch, w_E)=−0.17), and the interactions inherit the time trend (corr(z_E, trend)=−0.28). Rajan-Zingales designs work with hundreds of industry×country cells; 30 technologies with semi-collinear intensities cannot separate three cost channels. The fix would be more granular intensity measurement (e.g., engineering input shares per technology), not more econometrics.
