Results Memo — W9: The Dynamic Waterfall (Projected Realized-TFP Path, 2026–2040)

2026-06-10. Workstream W9 (RESEARCH_DIRECTIONS §9.4). Code: notebooks/28_dynamic_waterfall.py (consumes the W6 dataset, W7b wedges, W5 BTOS series). Outputs: notebooks/output/dynamic_waterfall/. Pre-registered spec: PAPER_NOTES.md §10 (locked 2026-06-10); one post-lock decision, disclosed below.

Question

W7b's waterfall is static accounting: 90% per-instance savings → measured wedges → a realized-now TFP band of [0.58%, 1.43%] at 9.3% incidence. Every number in that chain is a snapshot. What path does realized TFP take if each wedge moves at its measured speed — and when, under which parameterizations, does the implied TFP growth increment become large enough for the W2 detector to see?

Model

Anchored multiplicative index, no double counting:

S_b(t) = ceil_b(H(t)) × U_b(t) × g_succ(t) × g_auto_b(t),   b ∈ {lo, hi}
  • ceil_b(H) — W7b's wedge-adjusted ceiling restricted to tasks with human-only time ≤ H (the reliability frontier), aggregated through wage-bill weights as in W7b.
  • U_b(t) = min(A(t)·κ_b, 1) — utilization of the eligible frontier; A(t) is BTOS firm adoption, κ_b is calibrated so every path starts exactly at the static band at t₀ = 2026.38 (asserted against W7b's table in code).
  • g_succ, g_auto — quality and verification wedge multipliers (lower band only for g_auto).

κ has an interpretation, and it is a finding in itself (below).

Results

1. The grid's peak-increment years cluster at exactly two dates: 2027 and 2030.

  • 2027 cluster — coverage-driven. At METR's measured 131-day doubling, the 80%-reliability horizon crosses the entire AEI task sample (max human-only time ≈ 14 h) by mid-2027. Variants with an active coverage channel front-load a near-doubling of realized TFP: +1.4 pp/yr (lower bound) in 2027 even with wedges flat.
  • 2030 cluster — wedge-unlock-driven. With the coverage channel already saturated (50% gate), the verification-bound increment peaks at 2.2 pp/yr in 2030 under the unlock-by-2029 scenario.

2. Central paths (free-ceiling diffusion, 131-day doubling, 80% gate):

Scenario S(2030) S(2035) Peak increment Peak year
Wedges flat, verification lower bound 3.1% 5.3% 1.4 pp/yr 2027
Wedges flat, upper bound 8.1% 13.9% 3.8 pp/yr 2027
Unlock 2029, lower bound 6.2% 12.6% 2.2 pp/yr 2030
Unlock 2029, upper bound 9.6% 17.1% 3.8 pp/yr 2027

Conservative corner (flat wedges, L = 0.5 ceiling, no coverage channel, lower bound): S plateaus at 1.4%, peak increment 0.18 pp/yr — permanently invisible to the W2 detector. The spread between that corner and the unlock paths is the entire adjudication space, and each incoming AEI vintage and BTOS wave narrows it.

3. κ: the eligible-task margin is saturated today. Calibration implies adopters already utilize 93–99% of the 80%-reliability frontier. Growth cannot come from deeper use of currently-reliable tasks; it must come from adoption (extensive margin) and verification (δ). This is the dynamic restatement of Paper C's ordering of binding constraints.

(k_AI = 0.52 compresses further: 2027 / 2031 / 2048.) The unified paper's "mid-2030s, more plausibly 2050s" sentence anchors on raw lags and the old k; measured adoption speed selects the compressed branch — late 2020s to early 2030s for the steam/electrification analogies. The two clusters of the dynamic waterfall (2027, 2030) and the speed-scaled analogies (2028, 2031–34) land in the same window from independent machinery.

The falsifiable near-term prediction

The 80%-gate coverage channel predicts measurable acceleration in 2027 (+1.4 pp/yr lower bound, wedges flat). The quarterly Fernald detector (W2) adjudicates within ~8 quarters: if 2027 shows no such acceleration, the coverage channel is rejected and the no-coverage reading stands — everything rides on adoption × verification, and the earliest detectable increment moves to 2029–2030, conditional on the wedges moving at all. Under the stagnation reading (wedges flat, slow diffusion), nothing is ever detectable: the transition stays inside the measurement wedges.

Limitations

Capability paths are parameterizations, not forecasts — W9 transmits scenarios through measured wedges; it does not predict the unlock. Selection unwind (savings falling toward RCT ATEs as incidence broadens) is noted, not modeled: upper paths overstate. BTOS speed/ceiling confounding at 2.9 years of data. The 80%/50% horizon ratio (/5) and the 2029 unlock midpoint are the two free parameters; everything else is fit to data on disk.
